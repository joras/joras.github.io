advent of code 2019

joras
2019-12-10
aoc2019 aoc

Solving Advent of Code puzzles every christmas seems to be becoming a tradition. Last year was the first time I solved all the days, and this year I’ve set my goal to finish all the puzzles again. (but if something like day 15 of last year happens, I may postpone it - there is a limit of frustration I want to take)

This year has a bumpy start, the first 8 days I had a fever, and mornings were extra hard. Excuses excuses. But yeah, this year has been a year of silly mistakes:

  • Day 1 - easy, but I was slow
  • Day 2 - wrote solution fast, but forgot to convert opcodes to ints while loading file, took me a while to find the issue
  • Day 3 - forgot to convert to int again
  • Day 4 - I’m not even sure why it took me so much time to solve it
  • Day 5 - bunch of small and silly mistakes again
  • Day 6 - after last year I decided that I should graph functions, used networkX, but learning it took time.
  • Day 7 - technical debt, I did not clean up the computer code as planned, and I had to rewrite everything to support
  • Day 8 - trivial exercise, I have no excuses, small silly mistakes again,
  • Day 9 - stumbled on reading skill, took me a while to find out that relative writes are also possible
  • Day 10 - seemingly easy exercise. Got stuck for a while on float comparison, then at part 2 I messed up by mixing real and test data.

So, up to today, silly mistakes every morning, but most of the time I knew what to do. Things will be getting harder from now on.

I’ll add my repo too, nothing beatiful here https://github.com/joras/aoc2019
